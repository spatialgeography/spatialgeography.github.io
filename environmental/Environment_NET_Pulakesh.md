# Part 1: World Distribution of Plants (Floristic Kingdoms)

> **Floristic Kingdom** — a larger group of plants occupying almost the same environmental conditions

## Floristic Kingdoms of the World

### Australian Region
- **600 species**
- Genera and **Eucalyptus** — dominant plant (75% mass)
- Long, gaunt and sturdy plants

### Cape Region
- Southern tip of **Africa**
- Belongs to the category of **cryptophytes** — survive unfavourable conditions
- Most garden flowering plants: Laplia, Erica, Freesia

### Antarctic Region
- Patagonia, Southern Chile (South America) to New Zealand
- **Tussock grass**
- New Zealand: dominance of **Gymnosperms**
- Vertical strata of plant layers

### Palaeotropical Region
- Africa, South-West Asia, South Asia, South-East Asia, southern and middle China
- Sub-kingdoms:
  1. African Sub-Kingdom
  2. Indo-Malaysian Sub-Kingdom
  3. Polynesian Sub-Kingdom
- Key regions: West African Rainforest, Madagascar, Iran–Turanian, East Asian

### Neotropical Region
- South America except southern Chile and Patagonia
- Similarity with Palaeotropical region
- Separated by spreading of **Gondwanaland** → led to origin and development of new species

### Boreal Region
- Whole of North America (except Middle America & Canada), entire Europe, North Asia and Arctic region
- **Most extensive kingdom**
- Sub-regions:
  1. **RMR** — Rocky Mountain Region
  2. **ANAR** — Atlantic North American Region
  3. **ASAR** — Arctic and Sub-Arctic Region
  4. **ESR** — Europe Siberian Region
  5. **MR** — Mediterranean Region

# Part 3: Types of Plants (Ecological Classification)

| Plant Type | Adaptation |
|:---|:---|
| **Hydrophytes** | Adopted to aquatic environment (e.g., lotus) |
| **Mesophytes** | Adopted to average water and temperature; well-developed root system; normal temperature |
| **Xerophytes** | Adopted to dry habitat |
| **Cryptophytes** | Survive unfavourable/hostile conditions |
| **Cryptozoic animals** | Live beneath stones, logs, dead branches |

## Vegetation Zones (by Temperature)

| Zone | Temperature |
|:---|:---|
| **Megathermal** | Equatorial — high temperature year round |
| **Xenophytic** | Dry / arid zone |
| **Mesothermal** | 18°C to −3°C |
| **Microthermal** | Coldest month below −3°C |
| **Hekistothermal** | Tundra — lichens and mosses |

## Life Forms
- **Christen Raunkiær (1934)** — first gave the concept of life forms

# Part 4: Biomes

> **I.G. Simmons (1982):** *"The most extensive ecosystem unit which it is convenient to designate is called Biome"*
> A Biome = when all the plants, animals and soils are situated collectively in a region

## Classification of Biomes

```
BIOME
├── Land Biome
│   ├── Tundra
│   │   ├── Arctic Tundra
│   │   └── Alpine Tundra
│   ├── Temperate
│   │   ├── Taiga Forest
│   │   ├── Temperate Deciduous
│   │   ├── Temperate Grassland
│   │   ├── Mediterranean
│   │   └── Warm Temperate
│   └── Tropical
│       ├── Tropical Forest
│       ├── Savanna Biome
│       └── Desert Biome
└── Aquatic Biome
```

## Tropical Evergreen Forest (Selva)

- **High rainfall + high temperature** throughout the year
- **Latitude:** 10°N to 10°S
- **Regions:** Amazon Basin, Congo Basin, Indo-Malaysian region (Java, Sumatra, Borneo, Malaysia, Guinea)
- **6000–7000 species** of plants; **20,000 species** of flowering plants
- **70%** of total tropical evergreen forests

### Vertical Strata

| Stratum | Height | Description |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 1st (Dominant Layer) | 20–60 m | Uppermost layer |
| 2nd (Codominant) | 25–30 m | — |
| 3rd | 15–20 m | — |
| 4th (Herbaceous) | Up to 5 m | — |
| 5th (Ground Layer) | 0 m | — |

## Monsoon Deciduous Forest

- Three major areas: Neotropics (West Indies), Indo-Malaysian Zone, Eastern Africa & Northern Australia
- **Average rainfall:** 150 cm; 3 wet months
- **Plants:** 12–30 m height
- **Main trees:** Sal and Teak; Bamboo
- **Jhum Cultivation** practised here

---

## Savanna Biome (Aw Climate)

- Dominant **xeromorphic herbaceous plants**
- Regions: Llanos (Colombia & Venezuela), South-Central Brazil, Guiana, Paraguay, hilly areas of Central America
- **Rainfall:** 250–500 cm; Temperature not below 20°C
- **Vegetation:** African elephant grass
- **Animals:** Net Primary Product = 900 dry grams/m²/year
- **Marsupials** — tropical animals of South America
- No competition among animals of Savanna biome
- **A.F. Lamprey** divided savanna biome into **5 categories**

---

## Mediterranean Biome

- Also called **Sclerophyll ecosystem / biome**
- **Dry summer and wet winter**
- **Latitude:** 30° to 40°/45°N
- **Regions:** European land bordering Mediterranean Sea, Central & Southern California, Central Chile, NW coastal Africa
- **Animal community:** 201 vertebrate animals

### Local Names

| Region | Local Name |
|:---|:---|
| Southern Europe | **Maquis** |
| California | **Chaparral** |
| South Africa | **Fynbos** |
| Australia | **Mallee Scrub** |

- Vegetation: maquis community, fynbos, quagga

---

## Temperate Grassland Biome

- Located in **interior of continents**
- **Summer avg. temp:** 20–22°C; **Winter:** 1–2°C
- Considered **climax community** species
- Heartland of world dairy industry

### Regional Names

| Region | Name |
|:---|:---|
| Eurasia | **Steppes** |
| North America | **Prairies** |
| South America (Argentina) | **Pampas** |
| Australia (Murray-Darling basin) | **Downs** |
| New Zealand | **Canterbury Grassland** |
| Africa | **Veld** |

- Animals: **Kangaroos**
- **'Dust Bowl'** — extensive cultivation of semi-arid prairie regions of Great Plains, USA → enormous deflection of dry, loose, friable soil

---

## Taiga / Boreal Forest

- Also called **Temperate Coniferous Forest**
- **Siberian type of climate**
- Regions: Sub-arctic North America, Scandinavia Peninsula → Russian Siberia → Bering Sea; higher altitudes of Germany, Poland, Switzerland, Australia
- Rocky Mountains of North America
- Temperature **below 0°C for 6+ months**
- **Vegetation:** Hardwood and broad-leaved deciduous trees
- **Animal community:** Caribou and moose are most important

---

## Tundra Biome

- **Polar / Arctic climate**
- Found in North America and Eurasia between southern limit of boreal forest and the Arctic Ocean
- Parts of Alaska
- Mean annual temperature < 0°C; annual precipitation ~40 cm
- **Vegetation:** Lithosols (lichens and mosses); cryophytes
- **Animals:**
  - 1st category: Residential animals
  - 2nd category: Migratory animals
- **Eskimos** — established permanent settlement
- **Samoyeds** — other important tribes

---

## Marine Biome

- **2/3 of the total area** of biomes
- Up to 200 metres = **Photic Zone** (light penetrates)

# Part 5: Ecosystem

> **Ecosystem** = functional unit of environment (producer–consumer–decomposer relationship)

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|:---|:---|
| **Environment** | Everything in a region |
| **Habitat** | Area where an organism lives |
| **Biosphere** | Region on Earth that supports life = Lithosphere + Hydrosphere + Atmosphere |
| **Ecology** | From Greek *Oikos* (home) + *logos* (study) |
| **Biome** | A large naturally occurring community of flora & fauna |
| **Ecotone** | Transition zone between two biomes (e.g., mangrove) |
| **Edge Effect / Edge Species** | Species found at the ecotone zone |
| **Ecological Niche** | Unique functional role of a species in its habitat; no two species share the same niche |
| **Vermin** | Species that increase rapidly |

## Ecological Hierarchy

> Individual → Population → Community → Ecosystem → Biome → Biosphere

# Part 6: Trophic Levels & Energy Flow

> *'Trophe'* = Nutrient
> **Energy decreases** from first to last trophic level

## Trophic Levels

| Level | Organism | Food Source |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **1st TL** | Producers | Own food (plants, algae) |
| **2nd TL** | Herbivores | Eat producers |
| **3rd TL** | Carnivores | Eat secondary consumers |
| **4th TL** | Carnivores | Eat other carnivores |

- **A. Raymond Lindeman (1942)** — gave the concept of Trophic Level
- **August Thienemann (1926)** — coined 'producer' & 'consumer'

# Part 9: Ozone Depletion

- **Troposphere Ozone** → absorbs heat → **Bad Ozone**
- **Stratosphere Ozone** → acts as UV-B filter → **Good Ozone**

## Ozone Depleting Substances
- CFC (Chlorofluorocarbons)
- Nitrogen Oxide (NOₓ)
- Hydrocarbons

## Key Facts
- Ozone hole found **above Antarctica**
- 1% ozone lost → 2% more UV-B rays reach Earth
- **Chapman Mechanism:** Cl + O₃ → ClO + O₂

## Conferences on Ozone

| Conference | Year | Outcome |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Vienna Conference | 1985 | First conference to discuss ozone depletion |
| Montreal Protocol | 1987 | CFC reduction step by step |
| Helsinki Conference | 1989 | Strengthened Montreal Protocol |
| Saving the Ozone Layer Conference | 1989 | British Govt + UNEP; final withdrawal of all ozone-depleting gases |
