### Classical & Modern Theories
* **Weber's Least Cost Theory**: Locating industry at the point of minimum transport cost, considering material and labor indices.
* **Isodapane Analysis**: Lines of equal total transport cost — identifying optimal and sub-optimal locations.
* **Sargent Florence's Coefficient of Localization**: Measuring the spatial concentration of industries.
* **Losch's Profit Maximization**: Finding the location of maximum net revenue instead of least cost.
* **Smith's Spatial Margins of Profitability**: Defining the area within which industry can operate profitably.
* **Pred's Behavioral Matrix**: Decision-making under uncertainty — sub-optimal locations and information deficiency.
