## Rural-Urban Fringe

The rural-urban fringe is a transition zone between the city and the countryside, where urban and rural functions coexist and compete.

::: {.callout-note}
### Key Characteristics
* **Land Use Change:** The rural-urban fringe is most prominent for rapid **land use change** (conversion of agricultural land to residential, industrial, or commercial uses).
* **Demographic Shift:** Transition from rural to urban population characteristics.
* **Economic Dynamic:** High property values, speculative land holding, and development of commuter villages.
* **Social Complexity:** Fusion of rural traditions and urban lifestyles.
* **Exurb:** A low-density residential settlement developed beyond the suburbs.
* **Ethnoburb:** A suburban residential and business area with a notable cluster of a particular ethnic minority population (e.g., Asian migrants in Australia, Canada, NZ, and USA).
:::
