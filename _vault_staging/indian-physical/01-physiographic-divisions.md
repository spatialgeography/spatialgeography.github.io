### Physiographic Divisions (NET Notes — Pulakesh Pradhan)

- Mountainous (above 2135m) = 10.7%
- Hilly area (305-2135m) = 18.6%
- Plateau (305-915m) = 27.7%
- Plain land (0-305m) = 43%

#### Himalayan Mountains
- 2400 km long, 5 lakh sq km area; originated from Tethys Sea
- Formed from Late Cretaceous to Lower Pliocene
- **Trans Himalaya:** Zaskar, Ladakh, Kailash, Karakoram (Average elevation 3000m)
- **Great Himalaya (Himadri):** Average elevation 6100m, Mount Everest (8850m)
- **Middle Himalaya (Himachal):** Average elevation 3500-4500m, Pir Panjal, Dhauladhar
- **Outer Himalaya (Shiwalik):** Mio-Pleistocene sands, Duns and Chos
- **Longitudinal Divisions (Burrard):** Punjab (Indus-Satluj), Kumaon, Nepal (Kali-Tista), Assam
- **Glaciers:** Siachen (75 km), Fedchenko (74 km), Gangotri (30 km)
- **Passes:** Banihal, Khardung La, Zoji La, Rohtang, Shipki La, Nathu La, Bomdi La

#### Peninsular India
- 16 lakh sq km area, average elevation 600-900m
- **Aravallis:** Guru Sikhar (1722m)
- **Highlands/Plateaus:** Malwa, Bundelkhand, Baghelkhand, Chotanagpur, Meghalaya, Deccan, Telangana
- **Western Ghats:** Emerged upland ridge; Thal Ghat, Bhor Ghat, Pal Ghat
- **Eastern Ghats:** Palkonda
