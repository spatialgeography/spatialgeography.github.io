## Water Resources and Major Irrigation Projects in India

India has developed numerous multi-purpose projects to manage its water resources for irrigation, power generation, and flood control.

### Major Multi-Purpose Projects

1.  **Bhakra Nangal Project**: Located on the **Sutlej River** in Punjab/Himachal Pradesh. At 226m, it is one of the highest dams in India. The reservoir is called **Gobind Sagar Lake**.
2.  **Hirakud Project**: Located on the **Mahanadi River** in Odisha. It is the **World's Longest Dam** at 4801m.
3.  **Damodar Valley Project**: Located on the **Damodar River** in Bihar/Jharkhand and West Bengal. Based on the Tennessee Valley Project (TVP) of the USA.
4.  **Chambal Valley Project**: Located on the **Chambal River** in MP and Rajasthan. Includes Gandhi Sagar Dam, Rana Pratap Sagar Dam, and Jawahar Sagar Dam.
5.  **Rihand Project**: Located on the **Son River** in Mizapur (UP). The reservoir is called **Govind Ballabh Pant Sagar**.
6.  **Kosi Project**: Known as the "Sorrow of Bihar," located on the **Kosi River**.
7.  **Nagarjuna Sagar Project**: Located on the **Krishna River** in Andhra Pradesh/Telangana.
8.  **Tugabhadra Project**: Located on the **Tungabhadra River** in Andhra Pradesh and Karnataka.
9.  **Shivasamudram Project**: Located on the **Cauvery River** in Karnataka. One of the oldest older river valley projects in India (started in 1892).
10. **Farakka Project**: Located on the **Ganga River** in West Bengal. Helps remove silt for easier navigation and provides power.

### River Disputes: The Cauvery Dispute
*   States involved: Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Karnataka, and Pondicherry.
*   Background: Dispute over water sharing starts back in 1892. In 1924, an agreement was reached for the Krishnasagar Dam.
*   Apex Court Ruling (2007): Final allocation gave TN (56.62%), Karnataka (36.48%), Kerala (4.05%), and Pondicherry (0.94%).
