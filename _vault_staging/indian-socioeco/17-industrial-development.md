### Industrial Landscape
* **Major Industries**: Iron & steel, textiles, petrochemicals, IT, pharmaceuticals, and automobiles.
* **Industrial Regions**: Mumbai-Pune, Bangalore, Hooghly, Ahmedabad-Vadodara, and Chotanagpur.
* **Industrial Policy**: From License Raj to LPG reforms (1991) — privatization, globalization, and FDI.
* **Special Economic Zones (SEZs)**: Policy, performance, and spatial distribution.
* **Make in India & Atmanirbhar Bharat**: Contemporary industrial promotion strategies.
* **Cottage & Small-Scale Industries**: MSME sector — khadi, handicrafts, and decentralized production.
:::



### Industrial Development (NET Notes)

#### Phases of Industrial Development
- **Phase 1 (1950-65):** Large scale industries expanded (Mahalanobis Model in 2nd FYP)
- **Phase 2 (1965-1980):** Wage Good Model (C.N. Vakil); development slowed due to infrastructure/oil shocks
- **Phase 3 (1980-1991):** Industrial recovery
- **Industrial Policy 1991:** LPG reforms, public sector reduced from 17 to 8 industries, FIPB setup

#### Major Industrial Centers
- **Khetri:** Copper melting
- **Renukot:** Aluminium
- **Visakhapatnam:** Zinc melting, major ship building
- **Katni (MP):** Limestone quarrying
- **Mumbai:** Highest cotton mill concentration
- **Firozabad (UP):** City of bangles
- **Sindri:** Fertilizer production
- **Varanasi:** Diesel Locomotive Works
