## Measurement of Agricultural Productivity and Efficiency

Agricultural productivity is the measure of agricultural output per unit of input (land, labor, or capital).

### Key Techniques and Scholars

#### 1. M.G. Kendall (1939)
Proposed the **Ranking Coefficient Method**. He considered output per unit area (yield) across 48 administrative towns in England to determine productivity levels.

#### 2. Sapre and Despande (1964)
Used a similar ranking order method, giving weightage to the output per unit area along with the percentage share under each crop.

#### 3. Bhatia (1967)
Devised the **Yield Index** formula:
$$I_y = \frac{Y_c}{Y_r} \times 100$$
Where $Y_c$ is the hectare yield of a crop in the component area and $Y_r$ is the yield of the same crop in the entire region.

#### 4. L.D. Stamp (1967)
Introduced the concept of the **"Standard Nutrition Unit"** (SNU), measuring the carrying capacity of land in terms of the population it can support, taking caloric value into account.

#### 5. Endyedi (1964) and Shafi (1972-74)
Defined the **Productivity Index** to determine indices in respect of twelve food crops in India.
$$Index = \frac{Y}{Y_n} \div \frac{T}{T_n}$$

#### 6. Jasbir Singh (1972-74)
Modified previous techniques to calculate the carrying capacity of agricultural land in regions like Haryana, expressing it as a percentage of the entire region.

#### 7. V.R. Singh (1979)
Developed a technique concerned with delimiting agricultural productivity by computing intensity and spread indices of yield, grain equivalents, and cropping patterns.
