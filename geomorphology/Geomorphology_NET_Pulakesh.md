# Part 1: The Solar System

- **Nebula:** In space, thin gas forms clouds of enormous dimensions
- **Akashganga / Milky Way:** Our solar system belongs to this galaxy (= 100,000 million stars)
  - Light takes 8 minutes 32 seconds to reach Earth from the Sun
  - Nearest star next to Sun ⇒ Proxima Centauri (4.3 light years)
- **1 Light Year** = 300,000 km/s × 60 × 60 × 24 × 365 = **9,460,800,000,000 km**
- **Gravitational Pull of Sun** = 28 times higher than Earth's
- **Sun's composition:** 70% Hydrogen (H), 28% Helium (He)
  - Layers: Photosphere → Chromosphere → **Corona** (outermost layer)
- Sun's revolution speed: 220 km/second; one revolution ≈ 250 million years = **cosmic year**

# Part 6: Ten Fundamental Concepts of Geomorphology

1. **Uniformitarianism** — The same physical processes operating today operated throughout geologic time (though not always with same intensity). *"Present is the key to the Past"* — Hutton (1785), Playfair (1802)

2. **Geological Structure** is a dominant control factor in the evolution of landforms. W.M. Davis — *"Landforms are Structure, Process & Stage"*

3. Earth's surface has relief because **geomorphic processes operate at differential rates**

4. **Geomorphic processes leave distinctive imprints** on landforms; each process develops its own characteristic assemblage

5. As different **erosional agents** act on the earth's surface, there is an orderly sequence of landforms

6. **Complexity of geomorphic evolution** is more common than simplicity

7. **Little of the earth's topography is older than Tertiary**; most is no older than Pleistocene
   - 90% of present landscape developed in post-Tertiary
   - 99% is post-middle Miocene
   - Cincinnati arch & Nashville began to form

8. **Proper interpretation of present landscape** is impossible without appreciation of Pleistocene influences
   - Great Lakes = result of glacial modification
   - Ohio, Missouri Rivers = result of glacial modification of periglacial course

9. **Appreciation of world climates** is necessary for understanding varying importance of geomorphic processes

10. **Geomorphology attains maximum usefulness by historical extension**
    - 'Paleogeomorphology' → study of landform history (Bryan, 1941)

# Part 8: Interior of Earth

- **Kola Peninsula** — 7.5–9.5 km deep hole drilled to study interior
- **Crust** = outermost layer; *shells* = concentric layers below crust
- **Average density** of Earth = 5.17 g/cm³
- **Temperature** = 1°C per 32 meters depth
- At 40 km depth — pressure 1000 times atmospheric
- Magma chamber at 40–50 km depth

### Sources of Evidence

- Density, Pressure, Temperature, Volcanicity, Seismology

### Seismic Waves
- **P-wave (Primary):** 8 km/s to 14 km/s (1.7× faster than S-wave)
- **S-wave:** 4 km/s
- > *"Earthquakes are lamps illuminating the structure of the Earth's interior"* — Golitzyn

### Shadow Zone
- **1909** — Oldham: S-wave disappears at angular distance of 120°
- P-wave disappears at 103°–143°

### Earth's Layers (Discontinuities)
- Mohorovicic (Moho)
- Repiti
- Gutenberg
- Lehmann

# Part 35: Types of Erosion

### Attrition
- A form of coastal and river erosion
- Bed load is eroded by itself and the bed; rocks knock against each other → smaller and rounder

### Solution
- Water dissolves certain rock types (e.g., limestone)

### Abrasion
- Pebbles grind along river band and bed in a sandpapering effect

### Hydraulic Action
- Sheer power of water smashing against river bank
- Air trapped in cracks of bank causes rock to break apart

### Plucking (Glacial)
- Ice and glaciers move, scrape and pull away pieces of rock
