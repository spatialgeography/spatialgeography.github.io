### National Connectivity
* **Indian Railways**: Zones, gauge conversion, dedicated freight corridors, and high-speed rail.
* **National Highways**: Golden Quadrilateral, NHDP, Bharatmala, and expressway development.
* **Ports & Shipping**: 12 major ports, Sagarmala Project, and coastal shipping.
* **Civil Aviation**: Airport modernization, UDAN regional connectivity, and air traffic growth.
* **Telecommunications**: Digital India, BharatNet, and 5G rollout — bridging the digital divide.

### Transport Networks (NET Notes)

#### Roadways
- India has 55 lakh km road length (2nd after USA); 60% Pucca, 40% Kuccha
- **PMGSY (2000):** Launched by Atal Bihari Vajpayee
- **Longest Expressway:** Lucknow-Agra (302 km)
- **Bharatmala (2017):** 34,800 km road project, replaced NHDP
- **Bridges:** Dhola-Sadiya (Lohit River, 9.15 km)

#### Railways
- 68,000 km route, 1,15,000 km track
- **Longest Train:** Vivek Express (Dibrugarh to Kanyakumari)
- **Busiest Route:** Delhi to Howrah
- **Metro:** Kolkata (first), Bengaluru (Namma Metro), E. Sreedharan (Metro Man)

#### Waterways & Ports
- **National Waterways:** NW1 (Allahabad-Haldia), NW2 (Sadiya-Dhubri), NW3 (Kollapuram-Kollam)
- **Kandla:** First port developed after independence
- **Ennore:** First major private port
- **Visakhapatnam:** Deepest landlocked port

#### Pipelines & Airways
- **Pipelines:** HVJ/HBJ (1750 km), Urja Ganga (2540 km), Mundra-Delhi
- **Airways:** 1932 Tata Airlines (J.R.D. Tata) → Air India (1946)
