Statistical Geography

Quantitative methods, spatial statistics, and data analysis for geographic research.

Author

Geography Team

Welcome to the Statistical Geography module of Geography OpenCourseWare. This course covers the quantitative and statistical techniques essential for rigorous geographic research — from basic descriptive statistics to advanced spatial modeling.

UGC NET Syllabus

  • Sources of Geographic Information and Data (spatial and non-spatial).
  • Types of Maps, Techniques of Map Making (Choropleth, Isarithmic, Dasymetric, Chorochromatic, Flow Maps) Data Representation on Maps (Pie diagrams, Bar diagrams and Line Graph).
  • Applications of Measures of Central Tendency, Dispersion and Inequalities, Sampling, Sampling Procedure and Hypothesis Testing (chi square test, t test, ANOVA).
  • Time Series Analysis, Correlation and Regression Analysis.
  • Measurement of Indices, Making Indicators Scale Free, Computation of Composite Index, Principal Component Analysis and Cluster Analysis.
  • Morphometric Analysis: Ordering of Streams, Bifurcation Ratio, Drainage Density and Drainage Frequency, Basin Circularity Ratio and Form Factor, Profiles, Slope Analysis, Clinographic Curve, Hypsographic Curve and Altimetric Frequency Graph.

Inferential Statistics

Drawing conclusions about populations from sample data.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

ImportantHypothesis Testing
  • t-tests: Comparing means of two groups (independent and paired).
  • ANOVA: Analysis of variance for multi-group comparisons.
  • Chi-Square Test: Analyzing categorical data and goodness-of-fit.
  • Non-Parametric Tests: Mann-Whitney, Kruskal-Wallis, and Spearman’s rank correlation.
  • Confidence Intervals: Estimating population parameters with specified certainty.

Correlation & Regression

Analyzing relationships between geographic variables.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

TipBivariate & Multivariate Analysis
  • Pearson’s r & Spearman’s rho: Measuring linear and monotonic relationships.
  • Simple Linear Regression: Predicting one variable from another.
  • Multiple Regression: Incorporating several predictor variables.
  • Logistic Regression: Binary outcome prediction (e.g., land cover presence/absence).
  • Residual Analysis: Checking model assumptions and spatial autocorrelation in residuals.

Spatial Statistics

Statistics specifically designed for geographic data.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

NoteSpatial Analytical Methods
  • Nearest Neighbour Analysis: Testing for clustering, dispersion, or randomness in point patterns.
  • Moran’s I: Global measure of spatial autocorrelation.
  • Getis-Ord Gi*: Hot spot and cold spot detection.
  • Geographically Weighted Regression (GWR): Local regression modeling for spatially varying relationships.
  • Semivariograms & Kriging: Geostatistical interpolation methods.
  • Lorenz Curve & Gini Coefficient: Measuring inequality and spatial concentration.

Cartographic & Graphical Methods

Visualizing geographic data effectively.

TipData Presentation
  • Choropleth Maps: Representing area-based statistical data with color gradients.
  • Isopleth Maps: Continuous surfaces using isolines (isotherms, isohyets).
  • Proportional Symbol Maps: Circle size proportional to variable magnitude.
  • Histograms, Box Plots & Scatter Plots: Standard statistical graphics adapted for geographic data.
  • Triangular Diagrams: Three-variable compositional data representation.
