## Geographic Knowledge During the Ancient and Medieval Periods

The foundations of geography as a field of study were laid by ancient civilizations. Their contributions ranged from astronomical observations and mathematical calculations to descriptive accounts of people and places.

### Ancient Greek Scholars
The Greeks were pioneers in mathematical and descriptive geography.
- **Homer**: The father of geography; author of *Iliad* and *Odyssey*.
- **Thales of Miletus**: Proposed the first theorems of geometry; believed Earth to be a disk floating in water.
- **Anaximander**: Credited with drawing the first map of the known world to scale; used the Gnomon.
- **Hecataeus**: The father of prose; wrote *Ges-periodos* (Description of the Earth).
- **Herodotus**: Known for his historical and ethnographic geoinformation; "All history must be treated geographically."
- **Aristotle**: Laid the foundations of mathematical geography; argued for the sphericity of the Earth.
- **Eratosthenes**: Calculated the Earth's circumference almost exactly; termed the word "Geography."
- **Hipparchus**: Divided the circle into 360 degrees; developed the concept of latitude and longitude.

### Ancient Roman Scholars
- **Strabo**: Compiled the 17-volume *Geographica*, providing a comprehensive account of the Roman world.
- **Claudius Ptolemy**: Created the *Geographica* atlas; developed map projections (conic and stereographic).

### Ancient Indian Scholars
- **Varahamihira**: Discussed the shape of the Earth and its rotation in *Surya Siddhanta*.
- **Brahmagupta**: Contributed significantly to mathematical geography and base mathematics for navigation.
- **Aryabhatta**: Proposed that the Earth rotates on its axis and orbits the Sun.
- **Bhaskaracharya**: Discussed gravity and spherical trigonometry in *Siddhanta Shiromani*.

### Ancient Chinese Scholars
- **Zhang Heng**: Invented the world's first seismoscope.
- **Chang Chien**: Known for his explorations and reports on Central Asia (Silk Road).
- **Huan Tsang (Xuanzang)**: Detailed accounts of geography and culture in South Asia/India.
- **Zheng He**: Great explorer who conducted seven voyages covering the Indian Ocean.

### Medieval Arab Geographers
Arab scholars preserved and expanded upon Greek and Roman knowledge during the "Dark Ages" in Europe.
- **Al-Battani**: Improved calculations of the length of the year and planetary motion.
- **Al-Masudi**: The "Herodotus of the Arabs"; detailed descriptions of climates and cultures.
- **Ibn Sina (Avicenna)**: Contributed to geomorphology and climatology.
- **Al-Idrisi**: Created the *Tabula Rogeriana*, one of the most advanced world maps of the Middle Ages.
- **Ibn Battuta**: Traveled over 75,000 miles; wrote *Rihla* (The Travels).
- **Ibn Khaldun**: A pioneer of sociology and political geography; author of *Muqaddimah*.
- **Al-Biruni**: Expert in geodesy; calculated the Earth's radius and geographic coordinates.
