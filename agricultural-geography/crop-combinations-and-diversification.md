## Crop Combinations

Crop combination is the study of the diversity of crops grown in a specific area during a specific interval of time. It provides a basis for regional planning and understanding the complexity of agricultural regions.

### Major Statistical Methods

#### 1. J.C. Weaver (1954)
**Weaver's Minimum Variance Method** is the most famous statistical approach for identifying crop combinations. He compared the actual percentage of cropland under various crops with a theoretical distribution (e.g., 100% for 1 crop, 50% for 2 crops, etc.). 
*   **Formula**: $d^2 = \frac{\sum d^2}{n}$
*   Used to define the crop combination of 1081 counties in his research work.

#### 2. Weaver's Method Modifications
Several scholars modified Weaver's approach to overcome drawbacks like the tendency to show the lowest deviation for insignificant crops:
*   **Doi (1959)**: Introduced the concept of "Standard Deviation" to substitute Weaver's variance.
*   **Peter Scott (1957)**: Suggested that animals are as important as crops when determining the combination region of Tasmania.
*   **J.T. Coppock**: Modified methods to account for all animals on the farm by converting them into equivalent units based on food consumption.
