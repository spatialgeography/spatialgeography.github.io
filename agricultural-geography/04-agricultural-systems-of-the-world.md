## Whittlesey's Classification of Agricultural Regions (1936)

**Derwent Whittlesey** proposed a comprehensive classification of the world's agricultural regions based on five functional and structural criteria.

![Whittlesey's Classification of Agricultural Regions](Attachments/Whittlesey.png)

### Five Criteria for Classification

1. **Crop and Livestock Combination**: The specific mix of plants and animals being raised.
2. **Methods and Techniques**: The tools and practices used to grow crops and raise livestock.
3. **Intensiveness of Inputs**: The degree of application of labor, capital, and technology per unit of land.
4. **Disposal of Products**: Whether the produce is for subsistence or for sale (commercial).
5. **Ensemble of Structures**: The types of farm buildings and infrastructure used to house the operation.

### Major Agricultural Regions

1. **Nomadic Herding**: Extensive subsistence livestock raising (e.g., Fulani in West Africa, Masai in East Africa, Bedouins in Saudi Arabia).
2. **Livestock Ranching**: Commercial grazing of livestock over extensive areas (e.g., Pampa in Uruguay/Argentina, Veldt in South Africa, Downs in Australia).
3. **Shifting Cultivation**: Slash-and-burn agriculture (e.g., Milpa in Central America, Ladang in Malaysia, Jhum in North-Eastern India).
4. **Rudimentary Sedentary Tillage**: Tropical subsistence farming with fixed fields.
5. **Intensive Subsistence Agriculture**: Found in monsoon-dominated regions of Asia with high population density (paddy-rice dominant).
6. **Mediterranean Agriculture**: Specialized farming in Mediterranean climates (grapes, olives, citrus).
7. **Commercial Grain Farming**: Large-scale mechanized wheat/grain production (Steppes, Prairies).
8. **Commercial Livestock and Crop Farming**: Mixed farming aimed at the market.
9. **Commercial Dairy Farming**: Specialized milk and dairy product production (e.g., in Europe and North America).
10. **Specialized Horticulture and Olericulture**: Also known as "Truck Farming" (fruits and vegetables).
11. **Collective Farming**: Jointly owned/operated holdings (e.g., Sovkhozes in the former USSR).
12. **Commercial Plantation Farming**: Large-scale export-oriented monoculture (coffee, tea, rubber, sugar).
