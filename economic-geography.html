Economic Geography

Spatial patterns of economic activities, production, and resource distribution.

Author

Geography Team

Welcome to the Economic Geography module of Geography OpenCourseWare. This course explores how economic activities — primary, secondary, tertiary, and quaternary — are distributed across space and how location, resources, and human decisions shape economic landscapes.

UGC NET Syllabus

  • Factors affecting spatial organisation of economic activities (primary, secondary, tertiary and quarternary).
  • Natural Resources (classification, distribution and associated problems), Natural Resources Management.
  • World Energy Crises in Developed and Developing Countries.

Location Theories

Theoretical models explaining the spatial distribution of economic activities.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

TipClassical Models
  • Von Thünen’s Agricultural Model: Concentric rings of land use around a central market.
  • Weber’s Industrial Location Theory: Minimum transport cost, labor cost, and agglomeration.
  • Christaller’s Central Place Theory: Hierarchical arrangement of market centers.
  • Lösch’s Profit Maximization: Modifying Christaller’s model with demand and profit zones.
  • Perroux’s Growth Pole Theory: Economic growth radiating from key propulsive industries.

Primary Activities

TipResource-Based Economies
  • Agriculture: Subsistence vs. commercial farming, types of agriculture (intensive, extensive, plantation, mixed).
  • Mining: Metallic and non-metallic minerals, spatial distribution of mineral resources.
