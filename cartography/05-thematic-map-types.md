### Flow Map (NET Notes — Pulakesh Pradhan)

- Used to measure the degree of concentration of traffic, goods, and passengers along different lines of communication

---

### Choropleth Map (NET Notes)

- Suitable for showing economic and geographical phenomena
- Uses color or shading
- Color and shading should be chosen carefully for effective visual effect
- The variable is summarized over predetermined districts

---

### Chorochromatic Map (NET Notes)

- Shows non-quantitative areal distribution by shading and tinting
- Also known as the **colored or tint method** / **layering method**
- Non-quantitative areal distributions may also be shown using symbols such as dots, circles, triangles, or index letters like T for Tea and R for Rice
- From Greek terms related to area and color
- Also known as **area class maps** or **qualitative area maps**
- Map **nominal data** using symbols, colors, black and white, or patterns
- Colors mapped according to data boundaries
- Only nominal data should be graphed, with no indication of hierarchy or order
- Common examples: soil maps and biome maps

---

### Choroschematic Map (NET Notes)

- Shows non-quantitative areal distribution by symbols, index letters, and related devices
- Prepared when regional variations are to be conveyed effectively

---

### Chorochromatic vs Choropleth

| Feature | Choropleth | Chorochromatic |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Data type** | Quantitative (summarized over districts) | Qualitative (nominal) |
| **Boundaries** | Predetermined administrative districts | Drawn based on detailed locational data |
| **Representation** | Fields with graduated shading | Fields with colors/patterns |
| **Common ground** | Both represent fields; can be constructed from same data | |
