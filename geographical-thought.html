Geographical Thought

Evolution of geographic ideas from ancient civilizations to contemporary paradigms.

Author

Geography Team

Welcome to the Geographical Thought module of Geography OpenCourseWare. This course traces the intellectual history of geography as a discipline — from the earliest map-makers and travelers to modern critical and quantitative paradigms.

UGC NET Syllabus

  • Contributions of Greek, Roman, Arab, Chinese and Indian Scholars.
  • Contributions of Geographers (Bernhardus Varenius, Immanuel Kant, Alexander von Humboldt, Carl Ritter, Scheafer & Hartshorne).
  • Impact of Darwinian Theory on Geographical Thought.
  • Contemporary trends in Indian Geography: Cartography, Thematic and Methodological contributions.
  • Major Geographic Traditions (Earth Science, man-environment relationship, area studies and spatial analysis).
  • Dualisms in Geographic Studies (physical vs. human, regional vs. systematic, qualitative vs. quantitative, ideographic vs. nomothetic).
  • Paradigm Shift, Perspectives in Geography (Positivism, Behaviouralism, Humanism, Structuralism, Feminism and Postmodernism).

Ancient & Classical Geography

The earliest attempts to describe and understand the Earth.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

NoteFoundations of Geographic Knowledge
  • Greek Contributions: Eratosthenes (measurement of Earth’s circumference), Strabo (descriptive geography), Ptolemy (cartographic projections).
  • Roman Geography: Practical geographic knowledge for administration and military campaigns.
  • Indian & Chinese Traditions: Ancient Vedic cosmography, Chinese cartographic innovations.
  • Arab Geographers: Al-Idrisi’s world map, Ibn Battuta’s travelogues, Al-Biruni’s geodetic measurements.

Medieval & Renaissance Geography

The transition from theological to empirical understanding.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

TipAge of Exploration
  • European Voyages: Columbus, Vasco da Gama, Magellan and their geographic impact.
  • Cartographic Revolution: Mercator projection, globe-making, and the rise of atlases.
  • Bernhardus Varenius: Geographia Generalis (1650) — distinguishing general and special geography.

Modern Geographical Thought

The founding of geography as an academic discipline.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

ImportantThe German & French Schools
  • Alexander von Humboldt: Systematic physical geography, Kosmos, and the holistic view of nature.
  • Carl Ritter: Teleological approach and regional comparative geography.
  • Friedrich Ratzel: Anthropogeography, environmental determinism, and political geography.
  • Paul Vidal de la Blache: French school of genre de vie, possibilism, and regional monographs.
  • Determinism vs. Possibilism: The foundational debate shaping modern geographic methodology.

Quantitative Revolution

The mid-20th century shift toward spatial science.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

TipSpatial Analysis & Models
  • Logical Positivism: The philosophical basis for a law-seeking, model-building geography.
  • Central Place Theory (Christaller): Hierarchical settlement patterns and market areas.
  • Von Thünen’s Model: Agricultural land use as a function of distance from market.
  • Gravity Models: Predicting spatial interaction based on mass and distance.
  • Statistical Methods: Regression, factor analysis, and spatial autocorrelation in geographic research.

Behavioural & Humanistic Geography

Shifting focus from space to people and perception.

NoteSubjective & Experiential Approaches
  • Mental Maps: How individuals perceive and navigate geographic space.
  • Place & Sense of Place (Yi-Fu Tuan): Topophilia and the emotional bonds between people and places.
  • Phenomenology in Geography: Experiential understanding of lived space.
  • Behavioural Decision-Making: Bounded rationality and satisficing in spatial choices.
