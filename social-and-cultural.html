Social & Cultural Geography

Spatial dimensions of culture, identity, religion, language, and social structures.

Author

Geography Team

Welcome to the Social & Cultural Geography module of Geography OpenCourseWare. This course explores how culture, identity, religion, ethnicity, and social structures are distributed across space and how they interact with geographic landscapes and processes.

UGC NET Syllabus

  • Concept of Culture, Cultural Complexes, Areas and Region, Cultural Heritage, Cultural Ecology, Cultural Convergence.
  • Social Structure and Processes, Social Well-being and Quality of Life, Social Exclusion.
  • Spatial distribution of social groups in India (Tribe, Caste, Religion and Language).
  • Environment and Human Health, Diseases Ecology.
  • Nutritional Status (etiological conditions, classification and spatial and seasonal distributional patterns with special reference to India).
  • Health Care Planning and Policies in India, Medical Tourism in India.

Culture & Geography

The spatial expression of cultural systems.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

NoteCultural Foundations
  • Definition of Culture: Learned behavior, shared values, beliefs, customs, and material artifacts.
  • Cultural Diffusion: Carl Sauer and the spread of cultural traits — expansion, relocation, hierarchical, and contagious diffusion.
  • Cultural Regions: Formal, functional, and vernacular cultural regions.
  • Cultural Ecology: Human adaptation to environmental conditions — Julian Steward’s framework.
  • Acculturation & Assimilation: Cultural mixing, blending, and the preservation of identity.

Language & Religion

Two of the most powerful markers of cultural identity.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

TipLinguistic & Religious Geography
  • Language Families: Indo-European, Sino-Tibetan, Afro-Asiatic, Niger-Congo, and Dravidian.
  • World Languages: English, Mandarin, Spanish, Hindi, Arabic — as lingua francas and their geographic spread.
  • Endangered Languages: UNESCO Atlas — language death and preservation efforts.
  • World Religions: Distribution of Christianity, Islam, Hinduism, Buddhism, Sikhism, Judaism.
  • Sacred Spaces: Pilgrimage sites, temples, churches, mosques, and gurdwaras — their spatial significance.
  • Secularism & Religious Conflict: Geographic dimensions of communal tension and interfaith harmony.

Cultural Landscapes

How culture imprints itself on the visible landscape.

TipReading Landscapes
  • Carl Sauer’s Cultural Landscape: Culture as an agent shaping the natural landscape.
  • Vernacular Architecture: Building styles reflecting climate, materials, and cultural traditions.

Globalization & Cultural Change

How global connectivity reshapes culture and place.

ImportantCultural Globalization
  • Cultural Homogenization: McDonaldization, Americanization, and global consumer culture.
