## Key Facts at a Glance

- **Guyot** → Flat-topped sea mountain (also called Table Mountain)
- **Sequence of topographic features:** Continent → Shelf → Slope → Rise → Abyssal Plain
- **Strait** → Narrow strip of water joining two large bodies of sea water
- **Archipelago** → A group of islands
- **Dead Sea** → Called the *Salt Sea*
- **Red Sea** → Known as *Erythraean Sea* [Ocean Desert]; example of faulted structure
- **Submarine canyons** form due to **turbidity currents**
- **Suez Canal** connects Mediterranean Sea and Red Sea
- **Hypsographic curve** explains ocean bottom topography
- **1 fathom** = 2 metres = 6 feet = 1 nautical mile
- **Isobath** = line joining same depth
- **71%** of the total globe is covered by hydrosphere
- **Ridge vs. Rise:** Ridges have central rift valleys (e.g., Mid-Atlantic Ridge — 10 km wide, 3 km high walls); Rises have gentler topography and lack rift valleys
- **Ocean size (ascending):** Arctic → Indian → Atlantic → Pacific

# Part 2: Depth of the Ocean

- **Sonic Sounding Method** — geographical method to measure depth
- **Fathoms** — unit to measure ocean depth (1 fathom = 2 metres)
- **Hypsographic / Hypsometric Curve:** Kossinna (1921) first used; Sverdrup (1942) modified

## Ocean Floor Zones

| Zone | Depth | Key Statistics |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Continental Shelf** | Up to 100 fathoms / 200 m | 7.6% of sea surface area; Atlantic 13.3%, Pacific 5.7%, Indian 4.2%; Avg. slope 0.2° (17 ft/mile); sea level lowered 38 fathoms in Pleistocene |
| **Continental Slope** | 200–2000 m | Slope 6% (3°30'–2°2'); Avg. slope 4°17' (Shepard); 8.5% of total ocean area; Indian 5.6%, Atlantic 7.6%, Pacific 5.6% |

| **Deeps (Trenches)** | Below 6000 m | 1.2% of sea bottom; 57 total (Pacific 32, Atlantic 19, Indian 6) |

| Type | Description | Distribution |

# Part 11: Coral Reefs

- **Masses of limestone and dolomite**
- Confined between **25°N to 25°S**; within 60–70 miles
- Built by **coral polyps** — lime-secreting organisms
- Coral polyps are: green, yellow, pink, violet and white
- **Zooxanthellae** — give colour to corals
- Can form from Cambrian age
- Western coast of continents — **no coral reefs**
- Less than 1/10 of total ocean area

## Conditions for Growth

| Condition | Requirement |
|:---|:---|
| Temperature | 68–70°F (20°C) |
| Depth | Maximum 200–250 feet (60–70 m) / 150–170 fathoms |
| Water | Sediment-free |
| Salinity | 27‰ to 40‰ |
| Platform | Submarine bench (Cambrian) |

## Types by Nature & Mode of Occurrence

### Fringing Reef
- Narrow belt of reef along the steep shores
- Separated from shore by narrow shallow lagoon (0.3–1.5 m depth) = *boat channel*
- Examples: Sakau Island (Maskelynes, New Hebrides), South Florida Reef, Mehetia Island

### Barrier Reef
- **Largest and most extensive** among all reef types
- 45° outward slope of the reef
- Upper exposed part several miles wide
- **Great Barrier Reef** → Eastern Australia (9° to 22°S)

### Atoll
- Oval-shaped coral ring enclosing a lagoon
- Fiji Atoll, Truk Atoll (West Carolinas)
- **Funafuti Atoll** — important atoll of Ellice Island

## Types by Location

| Type | Description |
|:---|:---|
| Tropical Coral Reefs | Between 25°N–25°S |
| Marginal Belt Coral Reefs | 25°N–30°N and 25°S–30°S |

## Structure
- **Lagoon** — developed along margins of shore
- **Reef** — outer side of lagoon
- Sand Belt → Boulder Zone → The Reef

# Part 12: Tides & Tidal Waves

- **Pliny's *Historia Naturalis*** — tides developed due to combined action of sun and moon
- **Isaac Newton (1687)** — first rational explanation: Gravitational attraction
  - Acceleration of gravity = 0.000115 cm/sec²
- **Ratio of tidal forces:** Moon : Sun = **11 : 5**

## Key Tidal Concepts

| Term | Description |
|:---|:---|
| **Syzygy** | Sun–Moon–Earth in a line → extra tidal force → Spring Tides |
| **Quadrature** | Moon at right angle to Sun–Earth line → weaker tidal forces → Neap Tides |
| **Perigee** | Moon nearest to Earth → tides 20% above average |
| **Apogee** | Moon farthest from Earth → tides 20% below average |
| **Long periodic variation** | 1600-year cycle in tide-generating force |

- Earth's equator inclined at 23½°; Moon's orbit inclined at 5° to plane of ellipse

## Types of Tides

| Type | Interval | Cause |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Semi-diurnal** | 12½ hours | Two high and two low tides per day |
| **Diurnal** | 24¾ hours | One high and one low per day |
| **Quarter-diurnal** | — | Very exceptional |
| **Spring Tides** | Once a fortnight | Revolution & declination of moon (Syzygy) |
| **Neap Tides** | Once a fortnight | Moon in quadrature |
| **Monthly** | Monthly | Perigee and Apogee |
| **Equinoctial** | Every 6 months | Varying declination |
| **Yearly** | Annually | Perihelion and Aphelion |

## Theories of Tides

# Part 14: Sea Level Changes

## Types of Sea Level Change

| Type | Description |
|:---|:---|
| **Eustatic** | Change caused by change in the volume of water in ocean store |
| **Isostatic** | Local sea level change caused by change in level of land relative to sea |
| **Emergence** | Impact of a relative **fall** in sea level |
| **Submergence** | Impact of a **rise** in relative sea level |

## Causes of Sea Level Rise

| Cause | Rate |
|:---|:---|
| Thermal expansion | 1.2–1.6 mm/yr |
| Glacial / ice cap melting | 0.4 mm/yr (1961–1990); 1.0 mm/yr since 2001 |
| Greenland & Antarctic ice sheet melting | Significant contributor |

## Effects of Sea Level Rise
- Coastal flooding and inundation
- Storm surge events (Bay of Bengal, Hurricane regions)
- **Coastal erosion** — 70% of worldwide beaches are being eroded
- Current average: **3 mm/yr** sea level rise

| **Sverdrup (1942)** | Modified Hypsographic Curve |

| **Holland–Hansen** | T–S Diagram for water masses |
| **Shepard** | Average slope of continental slope (4°17') |
