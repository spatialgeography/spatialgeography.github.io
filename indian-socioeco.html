Indian Socio-Economic Geography

Socio-economic dimensions of Indian geography — development, disparities, and planning.

Author

Geography Team

Welcome to the Indian Socio-Economic Geography module of Geography OpenCourseWare. This course provides a comprehensive understanding of the socio-economic landscape of India — including population dynamics, urbanization patterns, industrial development, agricultural change, and regional disparities.

UGC NET Syllabus

  • Population Characteristics (spatial patterns of distribution), Growth and Composition, Determinants of Population, Population Policies in India.
  • Agriculture (Production, Productivity and Yield of Major Food Crops), Major Crop Regions, Regional Variations in Agricultural Development, Environmental, Technological and Institutional Factors affecting Indian Agriculture; Agro-Climatic Zones, Green Revolution, Food Security and Right to Food.
  • Industrial Development since Independence, Industrial Regions and their characteristics, Industrial Policies in India.
  • Development and Patterns of Transport Networks (railways, roadways, waterways, airways and pipelines), Internal and External Trade, Regional Development Planning in India.
  • Globalisation and its impact on Indian Economy.

India’s Population Dynamics

Demographic characteristics and trends.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

NoteDemographic Profile
  • Population Size & Growth: From 361 million (1951) to 1.4 billion — trends, projections, and demographic dividend.
  • Density & Distribution: Bihar, West Bengal, and Kerala vs. Arunachal Pradesh and Mizoram.
  • Sex Ratio: Child sex ratio concerns — Haryana, Punjab, Rajasthan vs. Kerala, Meghalaya.
  • Literacy: Interstate variations — Kerala (96%) vs. Bihar (62%) — gender and rural-urban gaps.
  • Religious & Linguistic Diversity: Scheduled languages, religious distribution, and the linguistic map of India.
  • Tribal Population: Scheduled Tribes — distribution, development, and rights (PESA, FRA).

Urbanization in India

Urban growth, challenges, and planning.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

TipUrban India
  • Census Definition: Statutory (municipal bodies) and census towns (population/density/non-agricultural criteria).
  • Urban Growth Trends: Million-plus cities — from 5 (1951) to 53+ (2021); megacities (Delhi, Mumbai).
  • Class I-VI Towns: Census hierarchical classification of urban centers.
  • Urban Problems: Housing shortage, slums, water supply, sanitation, traffic congestion, and air pollution.
  • Urban Planning: Smart Cities Mission, AMRUT, PMAY, and HRIDAY.
  • Urban Governance: Municipal corporations, municipal councils, and the 74th Constitutional Amendment.

Indian Agriculture

The backbone of India’s economy.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

ImportantAgricultural Landscape
  • Crop Seasons: Kharif (rice, jowar, bajra), Rabi (wheat, mustard, gram), and Zaid (watermelon, cucumber).
  • Irrigation: Canals, tanks, wells, tubewells, and the irrigation revolution — Green and White Revolutions.
  • Agricultural Problems: Fragmentation, lack of credit, price volatility, and farmer suicides.
  • Agrarian Reforms: Land reforms, MSP, PM-KISAN, Kisan Credit Card, and cooperative movement.
  • Agro-Climatic Zones: NARP (15 zones) — scientific regional planning for agricultural development.
  • Allied Activities: Animal husbandry, fisheries, sericulture, and horticulture.

Industrial Development

India’s industrial geography and policy.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

NoteIndustrial Landscape
  • Major Industries: Iron & steel, textiles, petrochemicals, IT, pharmaceuticals, and automobiles.
  • Industrial Regions: Mumbai-Pune, Bangalore, Hooghly, Ahmedabad-Vadodara, and Chotanagpur.
  • Industrial Policy: From License Raj to LPG reforms (1991) — privatization, globalization, and FDI.
  • Special Economic Zones (SEZs): Policy, performance, and spatial distribution.
  • Make in India & Atmanirbhar Bharat: Contemporary industrial promotion strategies.
  • Cottage & Small-Scale Industries: MSME sector — khadi, handicrafts, and decentralized production.

India’s connectivity infrastructure.

TipNational Connectivity
  • Indian Railways: Zones, gauge conversion, dedicated freight corridors, and high-speed rail.
  • National Highways: Golden Quadrilateral, NHDP, Bharatmala, and expressway development.
  • Ports & Shipping: 12 major ports, Sagarmala Project, and coastal shipping.

  • Telecommunications: Digital India, BharatNet, and 5G rollout — bridging the digital divide.

Regional Disparities & Planning

Addressing India’s development imbalance.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

ImportantDevelopment Planning
  • Inter-State Disparities: Per capita income variation — Goa, Kerala, Tamil Nadu vs. Bihar, UP, Jharkhand.
  • HDI Ranking: State-wise Human Development Index — education, health, and income dimensions.
  • Backward Region Development: BRGF, Aspirational Districts, and hill area development programmes.
  • Border Area Development: BADP for India’s peripheral and strategically important border regions.
  • North-East India: Special economic and development challenges — Look East / Act East Policy.
  • NITI Aayog SDG Index: Tracking India’s progress on Sustainable Development Goals by state.
