## Atmosphere: Structure & Composition



The Earth's atmosphere is a complex, dynamic gaseous envelope that sustains life and drives all climatic phenomena. Understanding its vertical structure and chemical composition is fundamental to climatology.

### Atmospheric Layers & Composition
* **Troposphere**: The lowermost layer (0–12 km), where almost all weather events occur. Temperature decreases with altitude at an average environmental lapse rate of 6.5°C per kilometer. It contains 75% of the atmosphere's mass and almost all its water vapor.
* **Stratosphere**: Extending from the tropopause up to 50 km, this layer contains the ozone layer (ozonosphere), which absorbs harmful ultraviolet (UV) radiation. Temperature inverts and increases with altitude here due to UV absorption.
* **Mesosphere & Thermosphere**: Above the stratosphere, the mesosphere sees temperatures drop sharply, while the thermosphere experiences extreme temperature increases due to solar radiation absorption. The ionosphere within this region reflects radio waves.
* **Atmospheric Composition**: The permanent gases include Nitrogen (78%), Oxygen (21%), and Argon (0.9%). Variable gases, though present in trace amounts, are critical for the greenhouse effect, including Carbon Dioxide (CO₂), Water Vapor, and Methane (CH₄).
