### Humidity, Clouds & Rainfall
* **Humidity**: Measured as absolute, specific, or relative humidity. Relative humidity is crucial for human comfort and cloud formation. Condensation occurs when air cools to its dew point, leading to fog, dew, or clouds.
* **Cloud Formation & Types**: Clouds form through convection (surface heating), orographic lift (forced over mountains), frontal wedging, or low-level convergence. High cirrus clouds indicate fair weather; layered stratus clouds bring steady drizzle; and towering cumulonimbus clouds are responsible for severe thunderstorms.
* **Types of Rainfall**:
  * *Convectional*: Intense local showers common in equatorial regions.
  * *Orographic*: Heavy rain on the windward side of mountains, leaving a dry 'rain shadow' on the leeward side.
  * *Cyclonic/Frontal*: Widespread precipitation when contrasting air masses collide.
* **Global Patterns**: High precipitation characterizes the ITCZ and monsoon domains; arid zones dominate the Subtropical Highs and deep continental interiors.
