## Insolation & Heat Budget



The sun is the primary engine of Earth's climate. The balance between incoming solar radiation and outgoing terrestrial radiation determines the average global temperature and climatic zones.

### Solar Energy & Earth's Energy Balance
* **Insolation**: Incoming solar radiation is fundamentally controlled by Earth's spherical shape and axial tilt. Latitude determines the angle of incidence, while the season determines the daylight duration. Atmospheric transparency also affects how much radiation reaches the surface.
* **Albedo**: This refers to the reflectivity of different Earth surfaces. Snow and ice have high albedos (reflecting up to 90% of insolation), while oceans and dark forests have low albedos, absorbing more heat. This plays a critical role in ice-albedo feedback loops.
* **Terrestrial Radiation**: The Earth absorbs shortwave solar radiation and re-emits it as long-wave infrared radiation. Certain atmospheric gases trap this long-wave radiation, creating the necessary greenhouse effect that keeps the planet habitable.
* **Heat Budget**: This represents the equilibrium between incoming and outgoing energy. While the whole Earth maintains a balance (ensuring it doesn't perpetually heat or cool), the tropics receive a surplus of energy and the poles operate at a deficit, driving global wind and ocean currents to redistribute this heat.
