## Industrial Location Factors


What determines where industries are established.

### Locational Determinants
* **Raw Materials**: Weight-losing (sugar, iron), weight-gaining (soft drinks, bakeries), and ubiquitous materials.
* **Power & Energy**: Proximity to coal, hydro, natural gas, and renewable energy sources.
* **Labour**: Quantity, quality, skill level, wage rates, and labor laws.
* **Transport**: Connectivity by road, rail, water, and air — minimizing transport costs.
* **Market**: Proximity to consumer markets, demand patterns, and market size.
* **Capital**: Availability of investment, banking infrastructure, and venture capital ecosystems.
* **Government Policy**: Tax incentives, SEZs, industrial corridors, and environmental regulations.
:::
