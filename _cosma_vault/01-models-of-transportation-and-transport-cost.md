## Models in Transportation

Models of transportation and land use help predict spatial interactions, development patterns, and the efficiency of transport networks.

### Classifications of Models

#### 1. Location and Accessibility Models
*   **Lowny Model (1964)**: The first significant model of transport and land-use purposes. It was basically demand-driven and used a gravity model to allocate activities.
*   **Central Place Model**: Modifications of the original central place models often serve as the basis for modern accessibility studies.

#### 2. Spatial Interaction / Interdependence Models
*   **Integrated Transport Land Use Package (ITLUP)**: Developed by **Putman**, it classifies models into three elemental things:
    1.  DRAM (Disaggregated Residential Allocation Model)
    2.  EAM (Employment Allocation Model)
    3.  TDM (Travel Demand Model)
*   **LILT**: Least Integrated Landuse Transport Model (Machett).
*   **MEPLAN**: Focuses on the interaction between market sectors and the transport network.
*   **TRANVS / PECAS / DELTA**: Advanced simulation models for urban and regional growth.

#### 3. Distribution and Flow Models
*   **Lowny-based Gravity Models**: Use gravity to allocate export industry workers to residences.
*   **IRPUD**: A model developed at Macro, Meso, and Micro scales to understand inter-regional services, new jobs, or job changes.
*   **Cellular Automata**: Used to explain complex behaviors in social and natural processes (vegetation, hydrology, soil) by considering neighborhood rules.
