### Agricultural Landscape
* **Crop Seasons**: Kharif (rice, jowar, bajra), Rabi (wheat, mustard, gram), and Zaid (watermelon, cucumber).
* **Irrigation**: Canals, tanks, wells, tubewells, and the irrigation revolution — Green and White Revolutions.
* **Agricultural Problems**: Fragmentation, lack of credit, price volatility, and farmer suicides.
* **Agrarian Reforms**: Land reforms, MSP, PM-KISAN, Kisan Credit Card, and cooperative movement.
* **Agro-Climatic Zones**: NARP (15 zones) — scientific regional planning for agricultural development.
* **Allied Activities**: Animal husbandry, fisheries, sericulture, and horticulture.
:::



### Agriculture, Food Security & Reforms (NET Notes)

#### Agricultural Scenario
- **Employment:** 58% population depends on agriculture; contributes 15% to GDP
- **Irrigation:** 55% sown area rainfed (60% land)
- **Land Holding:** 70% land holding less than 1 hectare
- **Agro-Climatic Regions (1989):** V. Nath (1951) gave basic concept; 15 major regions
- **Production Leads:** Mustard (Rajasthan), Maize (AP), Raw cotton (Gujarat), Wheat (UP)

#### Green Revolution
- **Norman E. Borlaug:** Father of Green Revolution
- **M.S. Swaminathan:** Father of Indian Green Revolution
- **HYV Seeds:** Kalyan Sona, Sharbati Sonara, Sonalika
- **BGRFY:** Bringing Green Revolution to Eastern India (2010-11)

#### Food Security & Land Reform
- **NFSA 2013:** Subsidized food to 75% rural and 50% urban population
- **TPDS (1997) & Antyodaya Anna Yojana (2000):** Subsidized food systems
- **Land Reforms:** Zamindari abolition, Tenancy reforms, ceiling on landholdings
