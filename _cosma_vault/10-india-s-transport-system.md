## Transport System of India

India possesses a vast and diverse transport infrastructure comprising roads, railways, airways, and waterways.

### 1. Road Network and National Highways
National Highways (NH) are the spinal cord of India's road network.
*   **NH1**: Delhi to Amritsar (via Ambala and Jalandhar).
*   **NH1A**: Jalandhar to Uri.
*   **NH2**: Delhi to Kolkata.
*   **NH3**: Agra to Mumbai.
*   **NH7**: Varanasi to Kanyakumari (longest highway in India).
*   **NH8**: Delhi to Mumbai (via Jaipur, Ahmedabad, Vadodara).
*   **NH9**: Pune to Machilipatnam.
*   **NH10**: Delhi to Fazilka (proceeding to Indo-Pak border).
*   **NH24**: Delhi to Lucknow.
*   **NH39**: Numaligarh to Indo-Myanmar border.

**Highway Numbering Convention**: 
*   Even numbers (2, 4, 6, ...) usually run North-South.
*   Odd numbers (1, 3, 5, ...) usually run East-West.

### 2. Expressways
*   **Mahatma Gandhi Expressway**: Ahmedabad to Vadodara.
*   **Yamuna Expressway**: Greater Noida to Agra (India's longest 6-lane controlled-access expressway).
*   **Mumbai-Pune Expressway**: First 6-lane high-speed expressway.

### 3. Railways
India has the 4th largest railway network in the world.
*   **History**: First railway proposal in Madras (1832). On 16th April 1853, the first passenger train was hauled from Boribunder (Mumbai) to Thane.
*   **Railway Zones**:
    *   **Central**: Mumbai (CST)
    *   **Eastern**: Kolkata
    *   **Northern**: New Delhi (Baroda House)
    *   **Northeast Frontier**: Maligaon (Guwahati)
    *   **Western**: Mumbai (Churchgate)
*   **High-Speed Corridors**: The Mumbai-Ahmedabad high-speed railway corridor (Bullet Train) was expected to begin in June 2018 with a target completion in August 2022.

### 4. Waterways
*   **Inland Waterways**: Significant for cargo movement, particularly in the Ganga and Brahmaputra rivers.
*   **Ports**: Crucial for international trade, with major ports on both the Eastern and Western coasts.
