## Geography and Federalism

Federalism is a system of government where power is divided between a central authority and constituent political units.

### Key Geopolitical Insights
*   **Neumann (1955)**: Defined Federalism as a device to "curb the evil use of power by dividing it among a number of competing power units."
*   **Geographical Basis**: Federalism often arises in states with large territories, diverse populations, or distinct regional identities that require a balance between unity and local autonomy.
*   **Case Example**: India's federal structure is deeply rooted in its racial, linguistic, and ethnic diversities.
