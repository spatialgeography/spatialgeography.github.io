## Concept of Nation and State

### 1. State
*   A **Political entity** with a fixed territory.
*   Derived from the Latin word **"status"**.
*   Defined by its political sovereignty. Famous quote: *"I am the state"* (**Louis XIV**).

### 2. Nation
*   A **Socio-cultural entity**.
*   Derived from the Latin word **"nasci"** meaning "to be born".
*   Implies common ancestry and a shared "place of origin" (homeland).
*   **Richard Hartshorne (1950)**: A nation represents a feeling of kinship and belonging together — an extension of the concept of family (in-groups vs. outsiders).
*   **John Stuart Mill**: A nation is a portion of mankind united among themselves by common sympathies which do not exist between them and any others.

### 3. Nationalism
*   The feeling of belonging to a nation.
*   The desire of cultural-linguistic and religious groups to achieve political status that would grant them measures of self-government.
