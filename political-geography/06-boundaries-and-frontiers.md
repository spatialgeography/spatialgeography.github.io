## Boundaries and Frontiers

In political geography, there is a distinct difference between "Boundaries" and "Frontiers."

### 1. Frontiers (Area)
*   **Nature**: Geographical and zonal in nature.
*   **Role**: An integrating factor between states on either side.
*   **Types**:
    1.  **Natural/Physical**: Mountains, rivers, forests.
    2.  **Ethnic/Anthropological**: Based on human demographics.
    3.  **Geometrical/Astronomical**: Based on lat/long.
    4.  **Political**: Zones of political influence.

### 2. Boundaries (Line)
*   **Nature**: Political and linear in nature.
*   **Role**: A separating factor that defines the limit of sovereignty.
*   **Evolution Process**:
    1.  **Arbitrary Process**: Drawn without regard to physical or cultural features.
    2.  **Evolutionary Process**: Developing over time through settlement and use.
    3.  **Arbitration Process**: Settled by legal or diplomatic third parties.

### Comparison
| Feature | Frontier | Boundary |
|---------|----------|----------|
| **Form** | Area/Zone | Line |
| **Integrity** | Integrating factor | Separating factor |
| **Orientation** | Outward-looking | Inward-looking |
| **Stability** | Fluctuating | Fixed by law |
