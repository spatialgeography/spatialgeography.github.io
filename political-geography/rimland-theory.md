## Rimland Theory (Nicholas Spykman)

**Nicholas Spykman** proposed the **Rimland Theory** in 1944 in his book *"Geography of the Peace"*, as a counter to Mackinder's Heartland-centric view.

### Key Assertions
*   **Sea Power over Land Power**: Spykman emphasized the importance of ocean navigation and the discovery of sea routes to India and America as fundamental facts of world politics.
*   **Defining the Rimland**: He renamed Mackinder's "Inner Crescent" as the **Rimland**. This amphibious buffer zone between the Heartland and the sea is the real key to world power.
*   **Geography of the Rimland**: It consists of the European coast land, the Arabian Middle-Eastern desert, and the Asiatic monsoon land.

### Strategic Dictum
Spykman reversed Mackinder's dictum:
> "Who controls the Rimland rules Eurasia;  
> Who rules Eurasia controls the destinies of the world."

### Relationship with Heartland
*   The Rimland has a central position with interior lines of communication.
*   The development of land transportation allowed the Heartland to begin competing with sea communication, but the Rimland remains the vital zone of contact and conflict.
