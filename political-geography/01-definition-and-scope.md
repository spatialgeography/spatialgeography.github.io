## Definition and Scope of Political Geography

Political Geography is the study of the spatial distribution of political processes and the ways in which these processes are affected by the physical environment.

### Key Concepts

*   **Territoriality**: This concept owes much of its origin to **Robert Ardrey's** book *"The Territorial Imperative"* (1966). He defined it as an area of space (water, earth, or air) which an animal or group of animals defends as an exclusive preserve.
*   **Geopolitik**: A term borrowed by **Karl Haushofer** from **Rudolf Kjellén's** book *"Der Staat als Lebensform"*. Haushofer defined it as "a science which deals with the dependence of political events upon the soil."
*   **Friedrich Ratzel**: Often considered the father of modern political geography, he viewed the state as a living organism (**Lebensraum**).
