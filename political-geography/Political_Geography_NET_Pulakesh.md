# Part 1: Sea Power Concept — Alfred Thayer Mahan (1840–1914)

- **Alfred Thayer Mahan** — American naval strategist
- Works:
  - ***The Influence of Sea Power upon History*** (1890)
  - ***The Life of Nelson*** (1897)
  - ***Problems of Asia*** (1910)
- Sea power provides a strategic model with explicit relevance to world politics
- Control of **sea lanes** = control of world trade and military power
- Believed that nations must have strong navies to become great powers
- **Kjellén's "geopolitical instinct"** complemented Mahan's sea power view

---

# Part 2: Heartland Theory — Halford J. Mackinder

> Mackinder was a **leading member of the Liberal Party** (British)

## Three Major Works

| Year | Work | Key Changes |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **1904** | *The Geographical Pivot of History* | Presented at Royal Geographical Society; Pivot Area concept |
| **1919** | *Democratic Ideals and Reality* | 'Pivot area' replaced by 'Heartland'; full geopolitical model |
| **1943** | *The Round World and the Winning of the Peace* | Modified Heartland; introduced 'Lenaland' and 'Mainland' |

---

## 1904 Model — The Geographical Pivot of History

*"In the present decade we are for the first time in a position to attempt with some degree of completeness, a correlation between the larger geographical and larger historical generalisation."*

### 4 Regions by Physical Formation
1. **Buddhism** — turned towards the Pacific
2. **Brahmanism** — towards the Indian Ocean
3. **Mahometanism** — turned towards both the Atlantic and the Indian Ocean
4. **Christianity** — in Europe, watered by the Atlantic from the west

> Together = **1000 million people** = **2/3 of the world's population**, coinciding with Five Seas

> *"If Junkie and Tartar nomads equipped only with the horse and camel could so shape the course of history in the peripheral land of the inner crescent, what might not a modern power, its mobility enhanced by the railroad, achieve in this respect?"*

---

## 1919 Model — Democratic Ideals and Reality / The Heartland

### Reasons for Revision (Political Changes)
1. Rising of Germany as a major continental power
2. Beginning of the war
3. Major part of Eurasia under German dominance
4. Emergence of communist system in Russia

### Key Developments
- Possibility of **Japanese controlling** the pivot area
- The **Steppe corridor** joins the Heartland with Eastern Europe
- Possibility of **Russians** organising and developing the Heartland
- **'Pivot area'** → replaced by **'The Heartland'**

### Heartland (1919 Definition)
> *"For the purpose of strategic thinking, the Heartland includes the Baltic Sea, the navigable Middle and Lower Danube, the Black Sea, Asia Minor, Armenia, Persia, Tibet, Mongolia"*

### Famous Quote
> **"Who rules East Europe commands the Heartland;**
> **Who rules the Heartland commands the World-Island;**
> **Who rules the World-Island commands the World."**

### World Island
> *"Euro-Asia plus Africa — consisting of two-thirds of the world's lands"*

> *"Mackinder's Heartland thesis seemed to indicate to the geopoliticians the necessity for a détente or even an alliance between Nazi Germany and the chief occupant of the Heartland, Soviet Union"*

---

## 1943 Model — The Round World and the Winning of the Peace (*Foreign Affairs*, 1943)

### Reasons for Second Revision
1. USA involvement in war
2. Russia responded to German attack; joined against Germany
3. Allied forces

### Key Changes
- Cut short size of Heartland
- Separated from Heartland: the part of Soviet Union lying east of River **Yenisei**
- Named it **'Lenaland'**: *"a rugged country of mountains, plateaus and valleys covered from end to end with coniferous forests"*
- **'Mainland'** = North Atlantic Ocean + Eastern United States + Western Europe

### Criticism of Heartland Theory
- Argued the policy of **'nuclear deterrence'** renders the Heartland concept obsolete
- Overemphasis on land power; ignored sea power
- Did not account for air power

# Part 3: Rimland Theory — Nicholas Spykman (1944)

> Work: ***Geography of the Peace*** (1944)
> **Opposed to land-based power** (counter to Mackinder)

*"The fundamental fact which is responsible for the condition of world politics is the development of ocean navigation and the discovery of sea routes to India and America."*

## Defining the Rimland
> *"Beyond the mountain barrier, the coastland region which is called by Mackinder the inner crescent may more effectively be referred to as the Rimland."*

## Characteristics of Rimland
- The inner crescent of **amphibian states** (land + sea power) surrounding the Heartland
- Three sections:
  1. **European coastland**
  2. **Arabian Middle-Eastern desert**
  3. **Asiatic monsoon land**

## Spykman on Heartland
> A central position with **interior lines of communication**, made powerful and unified by the development of land transportation to a point where it could begin to compete with sea communication.

## Spykman's Counter-Quote
> **"Who controls the Rimland rules Eurasia;**
> **Who rules Eurasia controls the destinies of the World."**

## Knik's View
- To him, the Heartland had played a **negative role** in history

- Studied the historical interaction between Heartland and Rimland regions

## Saul B. Cohen (1973)
- Work: ***Geography and Politics in a World Divided*** (1973)
- Offers a **hierarchical and regional world model**
- Allows traditional geographical concepts
- Divided world into:
  - **Geostrategic regions** (larger)
  - **Geopolitical regions** (smaller, within geostrategic)

# Part 4: Geopolitik — Karl Haushofer

> Haushofer borrowed the word **"Geopolitik"** from Kjellén's book ***Den Staat als Lebensform***

- **1924** — Haushofer started editing the journal ***Zeitschrift für Geopolitik***
- *The Institut* — eventually attached able geographers and workers in cognate fields
- Haushofer: *"The word 'politik' is not preceded by the prefix 'Geo' by accident. The prefix relates politics to the earth."*
- Definition: *"A science which deals with the dependence of political events upon the soil."*

## Rudolf Kjellén
- Swedish political scientist
- Coined the term **'Geopolitik'**
- Work: ***Den Staat als Lebensform*** (The State as a Living Organism)
- Conceived the state as a **biological organism** with needs for living space

# Part 5: Concepts of State, Nation and Nation-State

## State
- A **political entity** with fixed territory
- Comes from Latin word ***'status'***
- *"I am the state"* — **Louis XIV**
- **Four essential elements of a State:** Territory, Population, Government, Sovereignty

## Nation
- A **socio-cultural entity**
- Comes from Latin word ***'nasci'*** = *to be born*
- Implies **common ancestry** and **place of origin**

### Definitions of Nation

| Scholar | Definition |
|:---|:---|
| **Hartshorne (1950)** | *"A nation represents a feeling of kinship of belonging together — an extension of the concept of family, more properly an extension of the concept of in-groups versus outsiders"* |
| **John Stuart Mill** | *"A nation is a portion of mankind united among themselves by common sympathies which do not exist between them and any others"* |

## Nation-State
- A state whose political boundaries coincide with the cultural/ethnic boundaries of a nation
- Ideal political unit in modern times

## Nationalism
- A feeling of belonging to a nation
- The desire of cultural-linguistic and religious groups to achieve a political status giving them a measure of **self-government**

# Part 6: Territoriality

- Owes much of its origin from **Robert Ardrey's** book ***The Territorial Imperative*** (1966)
- *"An area of space, whether of water, earth or air, which an animal or group of animals defends as an exclusive preserve"*
- Concept extended from animals to human political geography

# Part 7: Boundaries and Frontiers

## Key Distinction

| Concept | Nature | Function |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Frontier** | Geographical in nature | **Integrating factor** between states on either side |
| **Boundary** | Political in nature | **Separating factor** between states |

> Frontier = Area | Boundary = Line

## Types of Frontiers (Area)
1. **Natural / Physical frontier** — mountains, rivers, deserts
2. **Ethnic / Anthropological frontier** — based on ethnicity and culture
3. **Geometrical / Astronomical frontier** — based on latitudes/longitudes
4. **Political frontier** — defined by political agreement

## Evolution of Boundaries (Line)
1. **Arbitrary process** — imposed without regard to natural or cultural features
2. **Evolutionary process** — developed gradually over time
3. **Arbitration process** — settled by mutual agreement or tribunal

## Classification of Boundaries

### By Origin (Jones, 1945 — 4-stage model)
1. **Allocation** — political decision to divide territory
2. **Delimitation** — boundary drawn on map
3. **Demarcation** — boundary marked on ground
4. **Administration** — boundary enforced and managed

### By Nature
- **Antecedent boundary** — drawn before area was settled
- **Subsequent boundary** — drawn after settlement
- **Superimposed boundary** — imposed by external power
- **Relic boundary** — no longer functions but evidence remains
- **Consequent boundary** — follows cultural/ethnic divide

# Part 8: Geography of Federalism

> **Neumann (1955):** *"Federalism is seen as one of the devices to curb the evil use of power by dividing it among a number of competing power units."*

## Key Features of Federal State
- Division of powers between central and state/provincial governments
- Written constitution
- Independent judiciary
- Dual citizenship (in some cases)

## Types of Political Organisation

| Type | Description | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Unitary State** | All power concentrated at centre | France, UK, Japan |
| **Federal State** | Powers divided between centre and regions | USA, India, Germany |
| **Confederation** | Loose union; member states retain sovereignty | EU (partially) |

# Part 9: Definition and Scope of Political Geography

## Definitions

| Scholar | Definition |
|:---|:---|
| **Ratzel** | *"Political Geography is the study of the state as an organism attached to the soil"* |
| **Hartshorne** | *"Political Geography is concerned with the variation of political phenomena from place to place in interconnection with variations in other features of the earth as the home of man"* |
| **Pounds** | *"Political Geography is the study of the variation of political phenomena over the earth's surface"* |

## Scope of Political Geography
- Geography of state boundaries and frontiers
- Geopolitics and strategic studies
- Electoral geography
- Geography of international organisations
- Geography of war and peace
- Political regions and regionalisation

# Part 10: Geopolitics vs. Political Geography

| Geopolitics | Political Geography |
|:---|:---|
| Applied / policy-oriented | Academic / theoretical |
| Studies world power relationships | Studies spatial variation of political phenomena |
| Associated with national interest and strategy | Objective, analytical |
| Mahan, Mackinder, Spykman, Haushofer | Ratzel, Hartshorne, Pounds |

# Part 11: World Political Map — Key Concepts

## Types of States by Shape

| Shape | Characteristics | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Compact** | Nearly circular; efficient administration | Poland, Cambodia |
| **Elongated** | Long and narrow; poor connectivity | Chile, Norway |
| **Fragmented** | Separated into parts | Philippines, Indonesia |
| **Perforated** | State surrounds another state | South Africa (Lesotho) |
| **Prorupt** | Compact + an elongated extension | Thailand, Namibia |

## Enclaves and Exclaves
- **Enclave** — territory of one state completely surrounded by another
- **Exclave** — territory separated from main body of state
