## Heartland Theory (Sir Halford Mackinder)

**Sir Halford Mackinder**, a British geographer and member of the Liberal Party, proposed the **Heartland Theory**, which emphasized the strategic importance of the interior of the Eurasian continent.

### Historical Context & Influences
*   **Alfred Thayer Mahan (1840-1914)**: Influence of sea power concept. Book: *"The Influence of Sea Power upon History"*.
*   **Rudolf Kjellén**: Concept of "geopolitical instinct".
*   **Mahan's "Problems of Asia" (1910)**: Provided a strategic model for sea power.

### Evolution of the Theory

#### 1. 1904: "The Geographical Pivot of History"
*   Presented at the Royal Geographical Society.
*   Identified the **Pivot Area** (Eurasia) as the center of global power, protected by distance from sea power.
*   Linked historical generalizations with geographical formations of four regions:
    1.  **Buddhism**: Towards the Pacific.
    2.  **Brahmanism**: Towards the Indian Ocean.
    3.  **Mahometanism**: Towards the Indian Ocean and the Mediterranean.
    4.  **Christianity**: In Europe, watered by Atlantic rains.

#### 2. 1919: "Democratic Ideals and Reality"
*   Replaced the term "Pivot Area" with **The Heartland**.
*   Included the Baltic Sea, Lower Danube, Black Sea, Asia Minor, Armenia, Persia, Tibet, and Mongolia.
*   **The Dictum**:
    > "Who rules East Europe commands the Heartland;  
    > Who rules the Heartland commands the World-Island;  
    > Who rules the World-Island commands the World."
*   **World-Island**: Europe, Asia, and Africa.

#### 3. 1943: "The Round World and the Winning of the Peace"
*   In response to WWII and USA's involvement.
*   Introduced the concept of the **Midland Ocean** (North Atlantic) and **Lenaland** (the rugged, forested part of the Soviet Union east of the Yenisei River).
*   **Mainland**: Consisting of the North Atlantic Ocean, Eastern USA, and Western Europe.

### Criticism
*   Argued that the theory was outdated by the policy of **nuclear deterrence**.
