About the Lab

Where spatial computing meets field-based geographic inquiry

Our Mission

The Spatial Geography Lab is committed to advancing geographic science through the integration of cutting-edge computational methods — deep learning, machine learning, and cloud-based geospatial platforms — with rigorous field-based survey research. We believe that the most meaningful geographic insights emerge when satellite-derived analyses are validated through systematic ground-truth observations, creating a complete research pipeline from data acquisition to actionable outcomes.

Vision Statement

To be a leading center of excellence in integrated spatial geographic research — combining the analytical power of deep learning, GIS, and remote sensing with the authenticity of field-based ground validation — empowering communities and decision-makers with reliable, evidence-based geographic intelligence.

Our Approach

What distinguishes our lab is our commitment to integrated research methodology:

  1. Computational Analysis First — We leverage deep learning (CNNs, vision transformers), machine learning (Random Forest, XGBoost, SVM), and cloud platforms (Google Earth Engine) for large-scale spatial analysis.

  2. Ground Validation Always — Every computational result is subjected to rigorous field validation through sequential exploratory research, GPS-based ground-truth campaigns, and systematic in-situ observations.

  3. Open & Reproducible — We develop our workflows in Python and R, share our code openly, and use open-source GIS tools (QGIS, GRASS GIS) to ensure reproducibility across the research community.

Core Values

Scientific Rigor

We uphold the highest standards of geographic research — every remote sensing product is accuracy-assessed, every model is validated, every conclusion is field-verified.

Open Science

We share our datasets, code, and methodologies openly. Our Python and R scripts, GEE workflows, and QGIS models are freely available on GitHub for the community.

Collaboration

We believe that impactful geographic research emerges from interdisciplinary teamwork — connecting geographers, computer scientists, ecologists, and planners.

Research Focus Areas

Our research operates across six interconnected domains:

  1. Deep Learning for Remote Sensing — Developing CNN, U-Net, ResNet, and transformer architectures for automated land cover classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection from satellite imagery.

  2. Machine Learning & Spatial Prediction — Applying Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, SVM, and ensemble methods for spatial modeling, habitat suitability, vulnerability assessment, and change prediction in Python and R.

  3. Google Earth Engine Analytics — Cloud-based planetary-scale analysis for time-series change detection, composite generation, spectral index computation, and large-area land monitoring workflows.

  4. GIS & QGIS-based Spatial Analysis — Advanced spatial analysis using QGIS, including multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA), network analysis, terrain derivatives, suitability modeling, and cartographic visualization.

  5. Field Survey & Ground Validation — Systematic field campaigns using GPS/GNSS equipment, stratified sampling designs, sequential exploratory research methods, and accuracy assessment protocols (confusion matrix, Kappa, F1-score).

  6. Python & R Geospatial Programming — Building reproducible spatial data science pipelines using GeoPandas, Rasterio, TensorFlow/PyTorch (Python) and sf, terra, caret/tidymodels (R) for spatial analysis and modeling.

Lab History & Milestones

2015

Lab Founded

Established with an initial focus on GIS-based spatial analysis and remote sensing applications using QGIS and ERDAS IMAGINE.

2017

Python & R Integration

Adopted Python and R as primary research languages, integrating GeoPandas, Rasterio, sf, and terra into all research workflows.

2019

Google Earth Engine Adoption

Began leveraging Google Earth Engine for large-scale, cloud-based remote sensing analysis, enabling continental-scale land monitoring.

2021

Deep Learning Research Program

Launched dedicated deep learning research, applying CNNs and transformers to satellite image classification and achieving state-of-the-art accuracy.

2023

Field Validation Protocols

Formalized our field survey and ground-truth validation methodology, establishing standard protocols for sequential exploratory research and accuracy assessment.

2025

Open Geospatial Platform

Launched an open-access platform sharing our datasets, trained models, GEE scripts, and QGIS workflows with the global research community.
