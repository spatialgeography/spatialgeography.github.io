Political Geography

Spatial dimensions of political power, borders, states, and geopolitics.

Author

Geography Team

Welcome to the Political Geography module of Geography OpenCourseWare. This course examines how political power is organized spatially — from the formation of nation-states and boundary disputes to contemporary geopolitical rivalries and electoral geography.

UGC NET Syllabus

  • Boundaries and Frontiers (with special reference to India).
  • Heartland and Rimland Theories.
  • Trends and Developments in Political Geography.
  • Geography of Federalism, Electoral Reforms in India, Determinants of Electoral Behaviour.
  • Geopolitics of Climate Change, Geopolitics of World Resources, Geo-politics of India Ocean.
  • Regional Organisations of Cooperation (SAARC, ASEAN, OPEC, EU), Neopolitics of World Natural Resources.

Core concepts and intellectual evolution.

NoteThe Political-Spatial Nexus
  • Definition & Scope: Study of the spatial expression of political processes and structures.
  • Friedrich Ratzel: Organic state theory, Lebensraum, and early political geography.
  • Semple & Huntington: Environmental determinism and its influence on geopolitical thinking.

State, Nation & Sovereignty

The fundamental units of political geography.

TipPolitical Entities
  • State vs. Nation: Territorial sovereignty vs. cultural identity; nation-states and multi-nation states.
  • Components of a State: Territory, population, government, and sovereignty.
  • Centripetal & Centrifugal Forces: Forces that unite or divide a state (nationalism, ethnic tension, federalism).

Boundaries & Frontiers

Lines and zones that define political space.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

ImportantBorder Dynamics
  • Types of Boundaries: Natural (rivers, mountains), geometric (latitude/longitude lines), and ethnographic.
  • Boundary Evolution: Antecedent, subsequent, superimposed, and relict boundaries.
  • Boundary Disputes: Territorial, positional, resource, and functional disputes.
  • Frontiers vs. Boundaries: Zones of transition vs. lines of demarcation.
  • India’s Borders: McMahon Line, Radcliffe Line, Line of Control, and Durand Line.

Geopolitical Theories

Grand strategies and spatial models of global power.

Get the Presentation ↗   |   Watch the Video ↗

NoteClassical & Modern Geopolitics
  • Mackinder’s Heartland Theory: Whoever controls East Europe commands the Heartland, and thereby the World Island.
  • Spykman’s Rimland Theory: The coastal periphery as the key to global dominance.
  • Mahan’s Sea Power Theory: Naval strength as the foundation of national power.
  • Cohen’s Geostrategic Regions: Shatterbelts and the hierarchical structure of global geopolitics.
  • Critical Geopolitics: Deconstructing geopolitical discourse and the power of representation.

TipSpatial Dimensions of Democracy
  • Electoral Systems: First-past-the-post, proportional representation, and mixed systems.
